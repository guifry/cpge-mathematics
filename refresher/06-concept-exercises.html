Complex numbers

Exercise 1 — Arithmetic

Compute $(3 + 4i)(2 - i)$ and express in the form $a + bi$.

Show solution

$(3+4i)(2-i) = 6 - 3i + 8i - 4i^2 = 6 + 5i + 4 = 10 + 5i$.

Exercise 2 — Division

Compute $\dfrac{5 + i}{2 - 3i}$ and express in the form $a + bi$.

Show solution

Multiply by the conjugate of the denominator:

$$\frac{5+i}{2-3i} \cdot \frac{2+3i}{2+3i} = \frac{(5+i)(2+3i)}{4+9} = \frac{10 + 15i + 2i + 3i^2}{13} = \frac{7 + 17i}{13} = \frac{7}{13} + \frac{17}{13}i$$
Exercise 3 — Modulus and argument

Find the modulus and argument of $z = -1 + i\sqrt{3}$. Write $z$ in polar form.

Show solution

$|z| = \sqrt{(-1)^2 + (\sqrt{3})^2} = \sqrt{1+3} = 2$.

$\cos\theta = -1/2$ and $\sin\theta = \sqrt{3}/2$. So $\theta = 2\pi/3$.

Polar form: $z = 2e^{i \cdot 2\pi/3} = 2\left(\cos\frac{2\pi}{3} + i\sin\frac{2\pi}{3}\right)$.

Exercise 4 — De Moivre

Compute $(1+i)^8$.

Show solution

First, polar form: $|1+i| = \sqrt{2}$ and $\arg(1+i) = \pi/4$. So $1+i = \sqrt{2}\,e^{i\pi/4}$.

$(1+i)^8 = (\sqrt{2})^8 \cdot e^{i \cdot 8\pi/4} = 2^4 \cdot e^{i \cdot 2\pi} = 16 \cdot 1 = 16$.

The answer is real! You can verify: $(1+i)^2 = 2i$, $(2i)^2 = -4$, $(-4)^2 = 16$.

Exercise 5 — Quadratic with complex roots

Solve: $\;x^2 + 2x + 5 = 0$.

Show solution

$\Delta = 4 - 20 = -16$. $\sqrt{\Delta} = 4i$.

$x = \dfrac{-2 \pm 4i}{2} = -1 \pm 2i$.

Check: the roots are conjugates (as they must be for a polynomial with real coefficients). Their sum is $-2 = -b/a$ and their product is $(-1+2i)(-1-2i) = 1 + 4 = 5 = c/a$. Vieta confirmed.

Combinatorics

Exercise 6 — Counting basics

A PIN code is 4 digits (0–9), digits can repeat. (a) How many PINs are there? (b) How many if no digit repeats?

Show solution

(a) Multiplication principle: $10^4 = 10{,}000$.

(b) First digit: 10 choices. Second: 9. Third: 8. Fourth: 7. Total: $10 \times 9 \times 8 \times 7 = P(10,4) = 5{,}040$.

Exercise 7 — Combinations

A committee of 4 must be chosen from 10 people. (a) How many committees? (b) How many if one specific person must be included?

Show solution

(a) $\binom{10}{4} = \dfrac{10!}{4!\,6!} = \dfrac{10 \times 9 \times 8 \times 7}{4 \times 3 \times 2 \times 1} = 210$.

(b) Fix that person in the committee. Choose the remaining 3 from the other 9: $\binom{9}{3} = 84$.

Exercise 8 — Binomial coefficient identity

Prove that $\displaystyle\sum_{k=0}^{n} \binom{n}{k}^2 = \binom{2n}{n}$.

Show solution

Consider choosing $n$ people from a group of $2n$ (split into $n$ men and $n$ women). The answer is $\binom{2n}{n}$.

Alternatively: choose $k$ men from $n$ (giving $\binom{n}{k}$) and $n-k$ women from $n$ (giving $\binom{n}{n-k} = \binom{n}{k}$ by symmetry). Sum over $k$:

$$\sum_{k=0}^{n} \binom{n}{k}\binom{n}{n-k} = \sum_{k=0}^{n} \binom{n}{k}^2 = \binom{2n}{n}$$

This is the Vandermonde identity (special case). The combinatorial proof is more illuminating than the algebraic one — you count the same thing two different ways.

Elementary statistics

Exercise 9 — Mean and variance

A data set has values: $2, 5, 7, 8, 13$. Compute the mean, variance, and standard deviation.

Show solution

Mean: $\bar{x} = (2+5+7+8+13)/5 = 35/5 = 7$.

Variance (using $\overline{x^2} - \bar{x}^2$):

$\overline{x^2} = (4 + 25 + 49 + 64 + 169)/5 = 311/5 = 62.2$.

$\text{Var} = 62.2 - 49 = 13.2$.

Standard deviation: $\sigma = \sqrt{13.2} \approx 3.63$.

Alternatively: $(2-7)^2 + (5-7)^2 + (7-7)^2 + (8-7)^2 + (13-7)^2 = 25+4+0+1+36 = 66$. Divide by 5: $13.2$. Same answer — the shortcut formula always works.

Exercise 10 — Probability with counting

A bag contains 5 red and 3 blue balls. You draw 3 balls without replacement. What is the probability that exactly 2 are red?

Show solution

Total ways to draw 3 from 8: $\binom{8}{3} = 56$.

Ways to get exactly 2 red and 1 blue: $\binom{5}{2}\binom{3}{1} = 10 \times 3 = 30$.

$P = 30/56 = 15/28 \approx 0.536$.

This is a hypergeometric probability problem. The structure is always the same: $\dfrac{\binom{\text{good}}{k}\binom{\text{bad}}{n-k}}{\binom{\text{total}}{n}}$.
