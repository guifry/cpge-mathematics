2. Differentiation rules

RuleFormula
Constant multiple$(cf)' = c \cdot f'$
Sum$(f + g)' = f' + g'$
Product$(fg)' = f'g + fg'$
Quotient$\left(\dfrac{f}{g}\right)' = \dfrac{f'g - fg'}{g^2}$
Chain rule$(f \circ g)' = f'(g(x)) \cdot g'(x)$

The chain rule in words: derivative of the outside (evaluated at the inside) times derivative of the inside.

Example: $\dfrac{d}{dx}\, e^{3x^2} = e^{3x^2} \cdot 6x$. Outside: $e^u$, derivative $e^u$. Inside: $3x^2$, derivative $6x$.

4. Quick patterns

When the inside function's derivative appears as a factor, the integral is immediate:

PatternResultExample
$\int f'(x) \cdot e^{f(x)}\,dx$$e^{f(x)} + C$$\int 2x\, e^{x^2}\,dx = e^{x^2} + C$
$\int \dfrac{f'(x)}{f(x)}\,dx$$\ln|f(x)| + C$$\int \dfrac{2x}{x^2+1}\,dx = \ln(x^2+1) + C$
$\int f'(x) [f(x)]^n\,dx$$\dfrac{[f(x)]^{n+1}}{n+1} + C$$\int 3x^2(x^3+1)^4\,dx = \dfrac{(x^3+1)^5}{5} + C$
