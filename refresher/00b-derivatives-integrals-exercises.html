Derivatives & Integrals Exercises

Refresher — 3 quick exercises

Reference: Derivatives & Integrals Cheat Sheet.

Exercise 1 — Differentiate

Find $f'(x)$ for each:

  1. $f(x) = 3x^4 - 2\sqrt{x} + \dfrac{5}{x}$
  2. $f(x) = e^{2x}\sin(x)$
  3. $f(x) = \ln(x^2 + 1)$
Show solution

(a) Rewrite: $3x^4 - 2x^{1/2} + 5x^{-1}$. Apply power rule term by term:

$$f'(x) = 12x^3 - x^{-1/2} - 5x^{-2} = 12x^3 - \frac{1}{\sqrt{x}} - \frac{5}{x^2}$$

(b) Product rule: $(e^{2x})'\sin(x) + e^{2x}(\sin x)'$.

$e^{2x}$ needs the chain rule: derivative is $2e^{2x}$.

$$f'(x) = 2e^{2x}\sin(x) + e^{2x}\cos(x) = e^{2x}(2\sin x + \cos x)$$

(c) Chain rule: outside is $\ln(u)$, derivative $1/u$. Inside is $x^2 + 1$, derivative $2x$.

$$f'(x) = \frac{2x}{x^2 + 1}$$
Exercise 2 — Integrate

Compute:

  1. $\displaystyle\int (4x^3 + \frac{1}{x} - \cos x)\,dx$
  2. $\displaystyle\int \frac{6x}{x^2 + 5}\,dx$
  3. $\displaystyle\int e^{-3x}\,dx$
Show solution

(a) Term by term from the table:

$$x^4 + \ln|x| - \sin x + C$$

(b) Recognise the $f'/f$ pattern: $f(x) = x^2 + 5$, $f'(x) = 2x$. The numerator is $6x = 3 \cdot 2x$.

$$3\int \frac{2x}{x^2+5}\,dx = 3\ln(x^2 + 5) + C$$

(c) $\int e^{ax}\,dx = \dfrac{e^{ax}}{a} + C$ with $a = -3$:

$$-\frac{1}{3}e^{-3x} + C$$
Exercise 3 — Definite integral

Evaluate: $\displaystyle\int_0^{\pi/2} \sin(x)\,dx$

Show solution

Antiderivative of $\sin x$ is $-\cos x$. Apply the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus:

$$\Big[-\cos x\Big]_0^{\pi/2} = -\cos(\pi/2) - (-\cos(0)) = 0 + 1 = 1$$

The area under one arch of $\sin x$ from $0$ to $\pi/2$ is exactly $1$.
