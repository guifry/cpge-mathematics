Sergei Bernstein

1880–1968

Russian

Approximation theory, probability theory, partial differential equations

Theorems in this course

  • Bernstein polynomials and constructive proof of Weierstrass approximation — Phase 5, page 8

Other notable contributions

  • Bernstein's inequality — if a trigonometric polynomial has degree $n$, its derivative's supremum is at most $n$ times its own supremum.
  • Solution of Hilbert's 19th problem — proved that solutions of certain elliptic PDEs with analytic data are themselves analytic (1904).
  • Bernstein's theorem on monotone functions — a function on $[0, \infty)$ is completely monotone iff it is the Laplace transform of a non-negative measure.

Sergei Natanovich Bernstein was born in Odessa in 1880 and studied in Paris, where he absorbed the French tradition of rigorous analysis. His doctoral thesis (1904) solved part of Hilbert's 19th problem on the analyticity of solutions to elliptic PDEs — a remarkable achievement for a young mathematician working on one of the great open problems of the day.

Bernstein's most lasting contribution to pure mathematics came in 1912, when he gave a beautifully constructive proof of the Weierstrass approximation theorem. Where Weierstrass's original argument was existential, Bernstein built explicit polynomial approximations — the Bernstein polynomials — using probability theory. The $n$-th Bernstein polynomial of a function $f$ on $[0,1]$ evaluates $f$ at points $k/n$ weighted by binomial probabilities, and converges uniformly to $f$. This was one of the earliest uses of probabilistic methods in analysis.

He also made deep contributions to probability theory itself, proving a version of the central limit theorem under weaker hypotheses than his predecessors. His inequality for trigonometric polynomials became a cornerstone of approximation theory. Bernstein spent most of his career in Kharkiv and later Moscow, training generations of Soviet mathematicians. He remained active well into his eighties, publishing important work on stochastic processes until shortly before his death in 1968.
